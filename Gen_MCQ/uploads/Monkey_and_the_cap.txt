In a small town lived a cap seller named Ramu. Every morning, he packed his bag with colorful caps. Some were red caps, some were blue caps, some were green caps, and some were yellow caps. He carried the bag on his shoulder and walked from village to village selling the caps.
One hot afternoon, Ramu was returning home after a long day. The sun was strong, and he felt tired. He found a big tree with cool shade and decided to rest for a while. He placed his bag under the tree, sat down, and soon fell fast asleep.
What Ramu did not know was that many monkeys lived on the branches above him. These monkeys were playful and curious. When they saw the capseller sleeping, they slowly climbed down. They opened the bag and took out all the caps. Each monkey wore one cap on its head and climbed back up the tree.
After some time, Ramu woke up and stretched his arms. He reached for his bag, but it felt empty. Shocked, he looked inside and saw that all the caps were gone. He searched around and suddenly looked up. To his surprise, he saw all the monkeys sitting on the tree branches wearing his caps.
Ramu became worried. He shouted, Give me back my caps. The monkeys copied him and shouted back. Ramu stamped his foot on the ground. The monkeys stamped their feet too. Ramu waved his hands in anger. The monkeys waved their hands the same way.
Then Ramu remembered something. Monkeys always imitate what they see. A clever idea came into his mind. He removed the cap he was wearing on his own head and threw it on the ground. As expected, the monkeys copied him. They removed the caps from their heads and threw them down too.
Ramu quickly collected all the caps and put them back into his bag. He looked up at the monkeys and laughed with relief. The monkeys jumped around happily as if they enjoyed the game.
Ramu tied the bag tightly and said to himself, It is good to stay calm and think. A clever mind can solve many problems. Then he picked up his bag and continued his journey home.
From that day onward, whenever he passed the same tree, he made sure to hold his bag tightly. He never forgot the lesson he learned from the monkeys.
